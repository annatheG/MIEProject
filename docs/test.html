Welcome to the Memory Experiment

Hi everyone! Thank you for participating in our study about the effects of short-term memory recall. This task will take approximately 8-10 minutes to complete. Please read the following instructions carefully before starting.

Overview

In this study, you will be asked to memorize a list of words and then recall them from memory.

This experiment consists of three trials, each containing three phases:

  • Memorization phase
  • Recall phase
  • Review phase
Your performance is based on how quickly you can recall as many words as possible and the number of words you recall correctly.

General Guidelines

  • Complete this task in a quiet environment without distractions.
  • Complete this task individually.
  • Do not write down, take pictures, or use external aids.
  • Use a computer or laptop.

Trial Procedure

Memorization Phase

Once you click "Proceed", a list of 15 randomly generated words will appear on the screen. You will have 40 seconds to study and memorize the list. After 40 seconds, the list will automatically disappear, and you will be taken to the recall phase.

Recall Phase

Type as many words as you can remember. Submit words by pressing "Enter". A timer will start at the beginning of the recall phase. Correct entries appear in their original positions. Incorrect words will not appear.

Review Phase

After submitting your responses, the original word list will reappear for review. Review time is 2.5 seconds multiplied by the number of correct words. After the review phase, the next trial will begin automatically (3 trials total).
